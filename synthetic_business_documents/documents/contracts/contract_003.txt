Dear Amit,

Following our discussions in Delhi last month, I'm documenting the key terms we've agreed upon for the collaboration between our organizations.

Parties Involved:
DataVault Technologies Pvt Ltd (represented by Amit Chawla, CEO) - the technology platform provider
InsightEdge Analytics LLP (our firm, represented by me, Neha Kapoor, Managing Partner) - the analytics and consulting partner

What we're agreeing to:
We'll work together on enterprise data analytics solutions for financial services sector. DataVault provides the platform infrastructure and we bring domain expertise, client relationships, and implementation capabilities.

Commercial arrangement:
Revenue split is 60-40 in our favor for projects we source and manage. For opportunities DataVault brings, split reverses to 40-60. Both parties invoice clients independently for their respective scopes. Minimum annual commitment from our end is Rs 5 crore in platform licensing fees regardless of project flow.

IP and proprietary stuff:
Platform remains DataVault's IP. Our methodologies, frameworks, and industry models remain ours. Any jointly developed solutions get shared IP with both parties having non-exclusive usage rights. Client data stays confidential to both parties.

Timeline and commitment:
We're looking at 3-year partnership starting April 2026. Either party can exit with 6 months notice after first year, though financial commitments for that year remain. We'll review performance semi-annually and can mutually adjust terms.

Non-compete understanding:
During partnership term and 18 months after, we won't engage competing platform providers for BFSI sector in India. DataVault won't directly approach our existing analytics clients without involving us.

Dispute resolution:
Any disagreements go through escalation (our MDs first), then mediation, and finally arbitration in Bengaluru if needed.

Let me know if I've missed anything or if you'd like changes. We can get the formal paperwork done once you confirm.
